# Powerbi_customer_journey_analysis
Customer Journey Analysis in Power BI – funnel analysis, conversion tracking, and repeat-user behavior insights.


## Project Overview

This project analyzes customer journey behavior using Power BI, focusing on funnel drop-offs, purchase behavior, and repeat visits.

The goal was to identify where users leave the purchase journey and translate the findings into actionable business recommendations.

---

## Tools & Skills

- Power BI
- DAX
- Funnel Analysis
- KPI Design
- Business Intelligence
- Data Visualization

---

## Dataset

The dataset includes website customer journey data across:

- 5,000 sessions
- 1,872 unique users
- 792 purchasing users
- Funnel stages: Home → Product Page → Cart → Checkout → Confirmation

---

## Business Questions

1. Where do users drop off in the purchase funnel?
2. When do most purchase sessions occur?
3. How does repeat-visit behavior relate to conversion?

## Key Findings

- Most purchase sessions happen within the first two visits, highlighting the importance of the early customer journey.
- The largest drop-off occurs between Product Page and Cart, where approximately 60% of sessions leave the funnel.
- Conversion rises significantly with repeat visits, from about 19% for one-session users to about 73% for users with 5+ sessions.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Improve the Product Page → Cart transition by reviewing product page content, pricing visibility, and add-to-cart flow.
- Reduce friction in the early customer journey, where most purchase sessions occur.
- Encourage repeat visits through remarketing, email reminders, personalized offers, and cart recovery flows.
